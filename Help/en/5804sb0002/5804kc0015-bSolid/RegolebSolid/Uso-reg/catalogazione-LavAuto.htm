Configure the rules for automatic machining operations

Description

The automatic machining operations are a set of configured machining operations starting from the geometries, to facilitate programming. Configuring an automatic machining operation means assigning rules to different types of geometry so that the system can apply them to the drawings created in the Cad/Cam environment, in order to automatically generate the probable machining operations.

Examples of automatic machining operations

NOTES:

Examples valid for woodworking machines.

Rule applied to points in order to generate borings

The rule defined below will be applied to all the points created on face 2 of the piece, with reference corner number 1.

362. Points drawn on face 2 of the piece

1. Click on the button to add a new rule.
The New rule dialogue box opens.
2. Click on the Point option on the Geometry selection page, then click on the button.
3. Click on the Boring machining operation on the Select the machining operation page, then click on the button.
4. Enter a short description, then confirm by clicking on the button.
5. In the "Rules" section, choose the "De Rule" option and, in the adjacent data fieldA defined area containing data or commands (data field or command field)., enter the following syntax: FCRN=”2(1)”.

FCRN is the parameter of the "Face and corners" data field; 2 indicates face number 2; 1 indicates the reference corner.

6. In the borings data areaGeneral term to define a specific area for data entry., set the following data in order to apply the rule to all the points:
• The "0: Diameter" option in the "Use tool" data field.
• A type of bit in the "Tool type" data field.
• The diameter in the "Diameter" data field, and the bore depth.
Rule applied to circles in order to generate borings

The following rule will be applied to all the circles created on any face of the piece with a radius of less than 14mm; these circles will be grouped together in a single machining operation, according to the diameter.

363. Example: 8 circles; 4 with a diameter of 5mm and 4 with a diameter of 8mm.

1. Click on the button to add a new rule.
The New rule dialogue box opens.
2. Click on the Circle option on the Geometry selection page, then click on the button.
3. Click on the Boring machining operation on the Select the machining operation page, then click on the button.
4. Enter a short description, then confirm by clicking on the button.
5. In the "Collect by" data field of the "Rule detail" section, choose the "Face" option.
6. In the "Collection rule" data field of the "Rule detail" section, enter parameter D.

This parameter indicates the diameter of the circle, and establishes that the bores must be grouped according to the diameter.

7. In the "Rules" section, choose the "De Rule" option and, in the adjacent data field, enter the following syntax: (R<14).

R is the parameter of the "Radius" data field of the circle, that must be less than 14mm in order for the rule to be applied.

8. In the borings data area, access the "Use tool" data field and select the Automatic option in order to apply the rule to all circles with a diameter less than 14mm. In this case, the tool is chosen automatically on the basis of the circle diameter. Define the bore depth as well.

For example, if four bores with a diameter of 5mm and four bores with a diameter of 8mm are drawn in the Cad/Cam environment (figure 2) and this automatic machining operation is applied, two boring operations will be generated: one grouping together the four 5mm-diameter circles and the other grouping the four 8mm-diameter circles.

Rule applied to lines in order to generate cuts

The following rule will be applied to a line that intercepts a rectangle.

364. Example: a line intercepting a rectangle.

1. Click on the button to add a new rule.
The New rule dialogue box opens.
2. Click on the Line option on the Geometry selection page, then click on the button.
3. Click on the Cut machining operation on the Select the machining operation page, then click on the button.
4. Enter a short description, then confirm by clicking on the button.
5. In the "Rules" section, choose the "Intersect Edges Rule" option.
6. TickClick on a check box to tick it and thereby enable that function. the box "Enable compensation".
7. In the cut data area, define the tool ("Tool name" data field) and the machining operation depth ("Depths" data field).

In this case, the line will be recognised as a cut (figure 3) because the "Intersect Edges Rule" option defined in the rule indicates that all the lines which intercept a closed geometry must be considered as cuts.

Rule applied to closed polylines in order to generate milling operations

The following rule will be applied to a polylineSet of consecutive straight lines, connected to each other at the ends., transforming it into a piece.

365. Example
A. profile geometry;
B. internal geometry;
C. result of the automatic machining operation applied.
1. Click on the button to add a new rule.
The New rule dialogue box opens.
2. Click on the PolylineSet of consecutive straight lines, connected to each other at the ends. option on the Geometry selection page, then click on the button.
3. Click on the Milling machining operation on the Select the machining operation page, then click on the button.
4. Enter a short description, then confirm by clicking on the button.
5. Enable the "Container geometry" box.
6. In the "Rules" section, choose the "Face Rule" option. Choose the “equal” (=) arithmetic operator, and enter the number 0 in the adjacent data field.

In this case, the profile polyline (ref. A, figure 4) that contains other geometries (e.g. ref B, figure 4) is transformed into a piece and profiled (ref C, figure 4).

Rule applied to polylines in order to generate engraving operations

The following rule applies an engraving to two polylines drawn on the zero face of the piece. In this case, draw the polylines on the 0 face of the piece and enter the word ENGRAVE in the "Geometry name" data field of both geometries. This name is useful, as it is used in the automatic machining operation in order to apply the engraving.

366. Example
A. geometries to be scored;
B. result of the automatic machining operation applied.
1. Click on the button to add a new rule.
The New rule dialogue box opens.
2. Click on the Polyline option on the Geometry selection page, then click on the button.
3. Click on the Milling machining operation on the Select the machining operation page, then click on the button.
4. Enter a short description, then confirm by clicking on the button.
5. In the "Rules" section, choose the "De Rule" option and, in the adjacent data field, enter the following syntax: NMC=”ENGRAVE”

In this case, the rule will be applied to all the geometries that have the word ENGRAVE in the "Geometry name" data field of the advanced cards.

6. In the milling data area, define the mill for making the engravings (Tool data field) and the depth of the machining operation (Depths data field).
