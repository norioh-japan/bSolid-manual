The standard faces of the piece

"Standard faces" (or sides) means the six basic faces obtained by creating a piece with the aid of the specific commandTool designed to perform a specific function/operation. For example, the “Print” command allows you to print a document. of the data areaGeneral term to define a specific area for data entry..

Description of the standard sides of the piece:

  •    side 0: upper part of the piece;
  •    side 1: left-hand part of the piece;
  •    side 2: front part of the piece;
  •    side 3: right-hand part of the piece;
  •    side 4: rear part of the piece;
  •    side 5: lower part of the piece.

Corners of the piece

Each side of the piece has corners as reference points (figure 2); these are the starting points for drawing or for inserting machining operations.

33.

The reference corners are used to establish the zero point from where to start in order to determine the co-ordinates of the geometries or machining operations; the drawing will change position according to the corner selected.
Figure 3 shows an example of two bores with the same co-ordinates, made by activating two corners: corner 1 and corner 2.

The non-standard faces of the piece

The non-standard faces are sides created by starting from open or closed geometric elements (lines, arcs, polylinesSet of consecutive straight lines, connected to each other at the ends., etc.). Depending on the type of geometry used, you can generate faces that are linear, circular, etc.
