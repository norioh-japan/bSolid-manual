Polyline

Description

Creates a figure made up of consecutive curved or straight segments, linked together at the ends. This tool is very handy for drawing tangent arcs within an outline context.

To generate the outline, use the relevant fieldsA defined area containing data or commands (data field or command field). of the data areaGeneral term to define a specific area for data entry..

Drawing an outline with straight segments
1. Click(1) Indicates the action of pressing (and immediately releasing) the mouse button on an object (icon, button, etc.) located under the pointer on the screen. (2) (verb) Pressing and immediately releasing the left mouse button to enable the function of a selected command. on the buttonCommand field that is responsive to mouse clicks and that enables operations to be activated. of the drawing barBar listing the drawing tools..
2. Click on one of the two line tools in the "Type of element" data fieldA defined area containing data or commands (data field or command field)..

3. Insert the start point of the outline by clicking in the drawing areaArea dedicated to the creation and modification of the geometries/machining operations..
To specify the start point, use the relevant fields of the data area.
4. Indicate the other end points of each single segment of the outline, by clicking in the drawing area.
5. Click on the point where you want to end the outline.
To close the outline, click on the button.
6. Click on the Undo button to close the PolylineSet of consecutive straight lines, connected to each other at the ends. card.
Drawing an outline with straight and curved segments
1. Click on the button of the drawing bar.
2. Click on one of the line or arc tools in the "Type of element" data field.

3. Insert the start point of the outline by clicking in the drawing area.
To specify the start point, use the relevant fields of the data area.
4. Move on to the various types of curved or straight segment by clicking on the relevant tools of the data area, then insert the end points of each single segment of the outline.
5. Click on the point where you want to end the outline.
To close the outline, click on the button.

6. Click on the Undo button to close the Polyline card.

Configuration

Type of element

PET

Used to select the type of geometric elementAn abstract element associated with specific properties: the point, line, plane and space are all geometric elements. of the outline.

Line

Creates a linear segment beginning from the absolute co-ordinates.

Line polar co-ordinates

Creates a linear segment beginning from the polar co-ordinates.

Tangent line

Creates a linear segment, tangent and consecutive to the previous segment.

Arc using 3 points

Creates an arc passing through three points.

Arc using 2 points and radius

Creates an arc using the radius of the arc and the co-ordinates of its start and endpoints as reference values.

Tangent arc

Creates an arc passing through three points and tangents and consecutive to the one which precedes it.

• An arc is said to be tangential to a line when its radius is perpendicular to the line and passing through the end point of the same. There is no vertex (corner) in the common point, rather there is continuity.
• An arc is tangential to another arc when the two have a point in common and the radii(window frames) Connection lines between the sections of the rough arc of the window frame. are located on the same line passing through this common point. There is no vertex (corner) in the common point, rather there is continuity.
