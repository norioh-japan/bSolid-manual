Inserting the suction cups or clamps on the carriages

To insert the suction cups, proceed as follows:
1. Click(1) Indicates the action of pressing (and immediately releasing) the mouse button on an object (icon, button, etc.) located under the pointer on the screen. (2) (verb) Pressing and immediately releasing the left mouse button to enable the function of a selected command. on the "Tab Supports" card located at the bottom.

3. Use one of the methods described in paragraph "Tooling the work table".
To insert the clamps, proceed as follows:
1. Click on the "Tab Uniclamp/Hyperclamp" card located at the bottom.
2. Choose the type of clampPressure exerted by the presser roller to hold the edgebanding strip in place. by clicking on one of the three side buttons.

The number of the button determines the size of the clamp.

3. Use one of the methods described in paragraph "Tooling the work table".

Removing the suction cups or clamps from the carriages

To remove the suction cups or clamps, use one of the methods described in the "Detooling the work table" paragraph.

Positioning the shapeable suction cups

Manual positioning
1. Use the cross cursorType of cross-shaped cursor. to select the carriage with the shapeable suction cup to be positioned.
2. Drag the carriage to the required position.
3. Once the carriage has been positioned, use the data areaGeneral term to define a specific area for data entry. to rotate the shapeable suction cup or insert a position: a valuePredefined / pre-set. Standard information useful for the control, if the requested information is not entered. or parametric expression (see "Measured positioning (parametric and non-parametric)").
Measured positioning (parametric and non-parametric)
1. Use the cross cursorMobile element that is visible on the screen, and which moves as the mouse is moved. to select the shapeable suction cup to be positioned.

In the data area, enter the position in data fieldA defined area containing data or commands (data field or command field). A. The position may be a simple numerical value or an expression for performing the parametric positioning.

To rotate the shapeable suction cup, use the relevant buttons B.

2. Choose the side (C, D or E) of the shapeable suction cup for calculating the position (i.e. the reference side which will be the starting point for moving it). In this case, the position entered refers to the chosen side.

Positioning the mobile panel supports

Manual positioning
1. Use the cross cursor to select the mobile support to be positioned.
2. Drag the support to the required position.
3. Once the support has been positioned, use the data area to enter a position: a value or parametric expression (see "Measured positioning (parametric and non-parametric)").
Measured positioning (parametric and non-parametric)
1. Use the cross cursor to select the mobile support to be positioned.

In the data area, enter the position in data field A. The position may be a simple numerical value or an expression for performing the parametric positioning.

2. Choose the side (B, C or D) of the support for calculating the position (i.e. the reference side which will be the starting point for moving it). In this case, the position entered refers to the chosen side.

B.  Indicates that the reference side is the left edge of the mobile panel support.

C.  Indicates that the reference side is the centre of the mobile panel support.

D.  Indicates that the reference side is the right edge of the mobile panel support.

Semi-automatic positioning

"Semi-automatic positioning" means the automatic movement and positioning of the work table objects beneath the piece.

In order to perform this operation in the best possible way, set the data in the relevant dialogue boxes (see: "Clamp positioning data"; "Shapeable cup positioning data ").

The positioner manages both EPS work tables (with automatic movement) and ATS work tables (with manual movement).

In this case, for the ATS work table, it is necessary to read the maximum positions that can be reached by the supports and carriages.
To tool the table, the positioner defines the position of the mobile panel supports and carriages (taking into account the area to be locked and the overall dimensions), and chooses the shapeable suction cups to be used, adjusting them so they lock the piece perfectly. When choosing the shapeable suction cups, it always favours the bigger ones (e.g. 132 x 146) rather than the smaller ones (e.g. 132 x 54).

To launch the semi-automatic positioning, visualise the Machine simulation environment and click on the button.

After positioning the piece on the work table, proceed as follows to manually position the clamps of the EKO machine:

1. Select the clamp from the tree listStructure containing a number of items, known as nodes, which in turn have branches and secondary branches like a tree. The node at the highest level is called the root. All the others derive from one single node and, in turn, can have other nodes..
