What are layers?

The layerLevels of a document created to carry out a drawing. (level) tools are used to organise the drawn objectsGeneral term used to define any drawing created in the drawing area (e.g. piece, texts, flat figures, etc.)., arranging them in groups that can be managed independently of others.

The use of levels can be handy to facilitate and properly organise work when creating complex documentsElectronic worksheet on which the geometric and technological parameters are inserted. containing many geometries and/or machining operations. For example, when the work is distributed across various levels and you want to modify only certain elements of the drawing (e.g. those placed on top of others), you can lock all the levels except the one relating to the object to be modified.

Each level assigns the following characteristics to the drawn objects:

  •    the colour of the base lines;
  •    the type of line (continuous, dotted, etc.);
  •    the thickness of the line;
  •    the display or otherwise of objects;
  •    the locking of objects (see also Locking/releasing a level);
  •    the material(nesting) Type of material to be associated with the articles and sheets of the nesting project. for the surfacesFigures of a three-dimensional area, that represent a thin open or closed structure..

Some characteristics of the drawn objects can be modified regardless of the level to which they belong; for example the colour and the display of certain objects on the level.

Choosing the level to draw on

When you draw, the created objects are automatically assigned to the active level (the one in the foreground of the drop-down menuList of options or instruments which expand vertically or horizontally when you click on the drop-down buttons. of the status bar).

With this drop-down menuList of commands (to be selected with the mouse) that develop a sub-menu, i.e. a list of options that allow certain functions/operations to be carried out., you can choose the level on which to draw, or assign a different level to the selected object.

  •    To choose the level on which to draw, click(1) Indicates the action of pressing (and immediately releasing) the mouse button on an object (icon, button, etc.) located under the pointer on the screen. (2) (verb) Pressing and immediately releasing the left mouse button to enable the function of a selected command. on the drop-down menu of the status bar and choose the level from the list offered.
  •    To move objects from one level to another, select them and then click on the drop-down menu of the status bar and choose the level from the list offered.

Locking/releasing a level

To lock a level so that the objects inside can no longer be modified, click on the open padlock on the status bar: the padlock will close .

The closed padlock indicates that the level is locked. The open padlock indicates that the level is released.

Creating a new level

1. Click on the buttonCommand field that is responsive to mouse clicks and that enables operations to be activated. of the status bar. The level management dialogue box will open.
2. Click on the New... button.
3. Specify the name of the level and the characteristics of the level objects in the open dialogue box.
Specify the material characteristics, using the A commands. Confirm the setting by clicking on button B.
4. Click on button Ok to confirm the creation of the new level and close the dialogue box.

Modifying and deleting a level

Modifying
1. Click on the button of the status bar. The level management dialogue box will open.
2. Select the table row corresponding to the level to be modified, then click on button Modify.
3. Make the necessary modifications in the modification dialogue box. Confirm the settings by clicking on button B.


4. Click on button Ok to confirm the modification in the levels table, then close the dialogue box.
Deleting
1. Click on the button of the status bar. The level management dialogue box will open.
2. From the table, select the row corresponding to the level to be deleted, then click on button Delete and confirm.

3. Click on button Ok to confirm the deletion of the level from the levels table, then close the dialogue box.
