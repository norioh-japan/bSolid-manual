Modifying the shape or size of the object

1. Make a double click to select the object to be modified; the object will turn red.
2. Position the cross cursorType of cross-shaped cursor. on the point of the figure or outline to be modified, then move the mouse. To enter values, use the data fieldsA defined area containing data or commands (data field or command field). of the individual geometries of the data area.

Some object handling procedures are explained below:

Duplicating objects by creating specular copies
1. Select the geometry to be duplicated.

2. Click on the button of the CAD options bar.
3. TickClick on a check box to tick it and thereby enable that function. the "Enable copy" box of the data area.
4. Click on the first point of reference, then drag the cursorMobile element that is visible on the screen, and which moves as the mouse is moved. to create the central axis for the copy. The copy will be shown in red.

5. Click on the second point of reference to establish the central axis. The copy will turn black.

Rotate the objects
1. Select the geometry to be rotated.

2. Click on the button of the CAD options bar.

To enable the copy, click on the Enable copy box of the data area.

3. Click on a point in the area to establish the centre of rotation.

4. Drag the cursor to create the rotated copy. The rotated object will be shown in red.

5. Click to lock the object rotation in the required point. The rotated object will turn black.

Scaling the objects
1. Select the geometry to be scaled.

2. Click on the button of the CAD options bar.

To enable the copy, click on the Enable copy box of the data area.

3. Click on a point in the area to establish the centre of the scale.

4. Drag the cursor to create the scaled copy. The scaled object will be shown in red.

5. Click in the required point to lock the resizing of the object. The object will turn black.

Inserting linear chamfers or connectors
1. Select the required geometry.

2. Click on the button to insert linear chamfersChamfering produces a corner. The chamfer can be linear or rounded., or on the button to insert curved chamfers (CAD options bar).
To enable the copy, click on the relevant box of the data area.
3. Click on the first side of the object. Set the dimensions of the linear chamfer or arc in the relevant data fields.

4. Click on the second side of the object; the object will turn black.

Selecting the objects

To select all the objects in the drawing areaArea dedicated to the creation and modification of the geometries/machining operations., click on the button of the data area.

When you select objects of the drawing area, the relative nodesFor the definition, refer to Tree list. are highlighted in the data area.

Select multiple objects with the cross pointer
1. Click on the button (cross cursor).
2. Press the left mouse key.
3. Position the cursor on the graphics and drag it to create a selection area around the required objects.
Select one object at a time with the cross pointer
1. Click on the button (cross cursor).
2. Click on the various objects you want to make active.
Select the geometries to be transformed into a piece or surface

There are different ways of selecting the geometries you want to transform:

  •    Select the geometries in the drawing area, choose the required drawing tool, then click on the symbol of the commands area.
  •    Choose the piece command or a surface too, then use the counter tool of the commands area to select the geometries in the drawing area.
  •    Choose the piece command or a surface tool, then select the geometries using the drop-down menuList of options or instruments which expand vertically or horizontally when you click on the drop-down buttons..

Deselecting the objects

To deselect all the selected objects, click on the button of the data area.

Deselect the objects with the cross pointer
1. Click on the button (cross cursor).
2. Click on the objects you want to deselect.

Removing objects

To delete objects from the drawing area, select them in the tree listStructure containing a number of items, known as nodes, which in turn have branches and secondary branches like a tree. The node at the highest level is called the root. All the others derive from one single node and, in turn, can have other nodes. or drawing area then use one of the following methods:

  •    Press the Canc key of the keyboard; the objects will be removed.
  •    Click on the button to remove the objects.

You can undo the last operations and thereby restore any objects that may have been accidentally removed. To do this, click on the button.

Cutting and copying the objects

To cut and copy the objects from the drawing area, proceed as follows:

1. Select the objects in the tree list or drawing area, then use one of the following methods:
• Click on the button to cut the objects. Alternatively, press the Ctrl + X keys of the keyboard.
• Click on the button to copy the objects. Alternatively, press the Ctrl + C keys of the keyboard.
2. Pasting the cut or copied objects (see Pasting the objects).

You can undo the last operations and thereby restore any objects that have been cut. To do this, click on the button.

Pasting the objects

To paste the objects in the drawing area, click on the button. Alternatively, press the Ctrl + V keys of the keyboard.

In this case, the objects to be pasted will appear in red. The cross cursor will appear, so you can choose their positions.

You can undo the last operations and thereby restore any objects that have been cut. To do this, click on the button.

Showing and hiding the objects

To show or hide the objects created in the drawing area, proceed as follows:

  •    Access the right-hand tree list and click on the light-bulb symbol to hide or show only certain objects belonging to a level.

A yellow bulb indicates that the nodeFor the definition, refer to Tree list. is active and that the objects it contains are visible in the drawing area.

A grey bulb indicates that the node is not active and that the objects it contains are not visible in the drawing area.

  •    To visualise or hide all the objects of a level, intervene on the light-bulb on the status bar.

When the bulb is lit up , this indicates that the level is active (the objects are visible).

When the bulb is switched off , this indicates that the level is not active (the objects are hidden).

Modifying the colour of the objects

The colour of the profile lines of objects belonging to a certain level is determined within the level itself. To modify the lines of certain objects, applying a non-standard colour, you must therefore use the tool.

Both the wireframe display and the photorealistic display are modified by taking the colour selected in the colour palette.

Proceed as follows:

1. Click on the button (cross cursor).
2. Select only those objects that you want to colour.
3. Click on the button of the status bar.
4. Choose the colour from the colour palette, then confirm.

The colour chosen from the colour palette will not be applied throughout the level, only on the selected objects.

The colours of the graphics

The colours of the drawn objects (figures, surfaces, etc.) are defined within the level or the colour palette (see Modifying the colour of the objects ).

The colours of the machining operations applied to the objects cannot be modified within the level; they depend on the settings made for the tool itself.
There are, however, two standard colours - red and green - that indicate the tool lead-in path (green) and the path of the tool at maximum speed (red).
