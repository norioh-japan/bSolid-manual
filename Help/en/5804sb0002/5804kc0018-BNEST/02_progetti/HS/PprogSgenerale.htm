Use sectioning optimiser

Enables (box tickedClick on a check box to tick it and thereby enable that function.) or disables the optimization of the positioning similar to the sectioning of rectangular pieces.

In this case, when the box is ticked, the profiles are considered as rectangular and their positioning on the sheet is optimised in order to take up as little space as possible.

This method can be useful in saving material(nesting) Type of material to be associated with the articles and sheets of the nesting project. when the form of the shapes to machine is similar to the rectangular shape.

The ticked box enables the data fieldA defined area containing data or commands (data field or command field). "Guillotine milling" shows only the field Additional margin in the card Filling-in and disables the card Sheets.

Guillotine milling

Enables (box ticked) or disables the introduction of the additional milling operations to perform cuts in x and y on the upper face of the piece.

The box enabledClick on a check box to tick it and thereby enable that function. allows to perform linear cuts.

Common cut

Enables (box ticked) or disables the optimization of the machining operations for all adjacent rectangular pieces.

In this case, all the sides in common between one geometry and the adjacent one are only machined once.

The ticked box disables the card Results cloning.

Consider the machining operations that protrude from the piece

Consider geometries only

Enables (box ticked) or disables the use of geometric profiles only without considering the machining operations.

By marking the box, the result of the processing produces only geometries without technological data.

Consider also geometries without machining operations

Enables (box ticked) or disables the use of all geometries in the program to be processed, even those without associated technology data.

In this way the result of the processing reports all the geometries present in the program, including those without machining operations.

Maintain machining operation sequence

Maintains the development sequence of the profiles and\or machining operations defined in each single document imported into the project.

By enabling this box, the order in which the profiles and/or machining operations were created is maintained, even during the project processing phase.

Separate small pieces first

Enables (box ticked) or disables the arrangement of the machining operations of closed external profiles on the basis of their size.

If the box is ticked, this means the closed external profiles are machined in increasing order, i.e. from the smallest to the biggest.

By enabling the "Maintain machining operation sequence" box too, the machining operations on closed external profiles will all be grouped together at the end (after the internal ones), in increasing order.

The fields are disabled when the box "Guillotine milling"is ticked.

Guillotine cut

Card Milling and card Advanced only show the specific data useful for the programming of this type of milling; for their description, see Milling (environmentCad/Cam).

The following data allows to generate additional milling operations, called Guillotine cut.

Additional incoming length

Additional length outside the panel/sheet, referring to the starting point of the geometry to be machined, to anticipate the beginning of the milling operation.

Additional outgoing length

Additional length outside the panel/sheet, referring to the finish point of the geometry to be machined, to postpone the end of milling operation.

Remove grazing millings on left side

Enables (box ticked) or disables the deletion of the milling on the left side of the piece to be machined, in the event that no double edge trim has been defined for that side.

Remove grazing millings on top side

Enables (box ticked) or disables the deletion of the milling on the rear side of the piece to be machined, in the event that no double edge trim has been defined for that side.

Remove grazing millings on right side

Enables (box ticked) or disables the deletion of the milling on the right side of the piece to be machined, in the event that no double edge trim has been defined for that side.

Remove grazing millings on bottom side

Enables (box ticked) or disables the deletion of the milling on the front side of the piece to be machined, in the event that no double edge trim has been defined for that side.

Depth levels separation

Enables (box ticked) or disables the subsequent data field that allows to manage how to perform the steps defined in the data fieldVertical movements (see Milling , environment Cad/Cam).

By ticking the box the steps are distributed throughout the panel; that is, if two steps have been programmed, it means that the tool passes once on all the paths until the entire surfaceFigures of a three-dimensional area, that represent a thin open or closed structure. of the panel is machined and then a second time.

By leaving the box unticked the steps are broken; that is, if two steps have been programmed, it means that the tool makes two passes on the first path, then two on the second, on the third and son on, until the entire surface of the panel is machined.

Machining speed for last step
