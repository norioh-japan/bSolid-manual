Configure the rules for the macro

Description

The macros are sub-programs to be recalled in the Cad/Cam environment, to facilitate programming.

Creating a macroProgram part created to execute certain instructions automatically. Can be called up when needed. Once it has been launched, a macro exploits the program interface to perform a sequence of operations programmed by the user. means generating a documentElectronic worksheet on which the geometric and technological parameters are inserted. containing geometric and technological data, saving it as a macro, and managing it by means of the relevant macro management card.

Open and modify the macro

The macros can be modified in various ways (modifying the contents, adding geometries and/or machining operations in the Cad/Cam environment, or modifying the structure and the applicationA synonym of "program" - software with the specific task of managing data, graphics, etc. and/or machining parameters) directly in the macro management card.

To open a macro and visualise its contents in the Cad/Cam environment, proceed as follows:
1. Access the bSolid rules environment and click on the button.
2. Select the macro to be modified from the table.
3. Click on the button.
4. Make the necessary modifications and overwrite the macro by clicking on the button.
To open a macro in order to modify its values or variables, proceed as follows:
1. Access the bSolid rules environment and click on the button.
2. Click twice on the table row showing the name of the macro you want to modify. The card with the macro data will open.
3. Modify the data, then save.
