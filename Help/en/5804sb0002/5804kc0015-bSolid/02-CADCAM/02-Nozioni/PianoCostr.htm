Construction plane

The construction planePlane on which a flat geometry is constructed. The default plane is the XY plane. can be likened to a worksheet on which the cursorMobile element that is visible on the screen, and which moves as the mouse is moved. is moved in order to draw.

It has an origin, a system of co-ordinates (see Coordinate system), and a gridA network of lines. For a detailed description, see the paragraph dealing with the grid. (see The grid).

The construction plane can be set with any direction, using the specific commands of the orthogonal viewsEnables an object to be displayed from any point of space. (see Orthogonal view tools). The predefined view of the construction plane is generally the upper one.

To change the construction plane, use the specific tools of the standard bar (see Orthogonal view tools).

Coordinate system

The co-ordinate system used to identify the position of a point on the plane or in space is the Cartesian one: x, y and z.

25. Co-ordinates system for drawing two-dimensional figures

The zero origin is conventionally placed in the middle of the drawing areaArea dedicated to the creation and modification of the geometries/machining operations., and hence of the construction plane grid.

26. Zero origin of the construction planes

To enter the data, you can use three different types of co-ordinate:

  •    absolute co-ordinates;
  •    relative co-ordinates (on the basis of the last point inserted);
  •    polar co-ordinates (length and angle).

The valuePredefined / pre-set. Standard information useful for the control, if the requested information is not entered. of the co-ordinates of the point where the mouse cursor is located can be seen in the status bar.

The grid

A network of lines with regular centre distances, that takes up a specific part of the construction plane. The grid provides a visual aid while you are drawing.

The centre of the grid is the zero origin of the chosen construction plane (the point of intersection of the two perpendicular red lines).

4. In the Snap step data field, define the snap gaps (i.e. the gap between one point and the next) for attracting the cursor to the grid.

The snap gaps need not necessarily correspond to the distance between the points of the grid. For example, in the Grid step field you can set a higher value to be used as a reference, and leave a smaller gap in the Snap step field, to guarantee greater drawing precision.

Visualising and hiding the grid

  •    To display the grid, click on the Gridbutton on the status bar. The button will light up.
  •    To hide the grid, click on the Gridbutton on the status bar. The button will turn black.
