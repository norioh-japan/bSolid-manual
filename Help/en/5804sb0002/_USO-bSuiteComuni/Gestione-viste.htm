Rotate the view

With the command:

1. Click(1) Indicates the action of pressing (and immediately releasing) the mouse button on an object (icon, button, etc.) located under the pointer on the screen. (2) (verb) Pressing and immediately releasing the left mouse button to enable the function of a selected command. on the buttonCommand field that is responsive to mouse clicks and that enables operations to be activated..
2. Press the left mouse key.
3. Position the cursorMobile element that is visible on the screen, and which moves as the mouse is moved. on the graphic, then move it.

With the mouse:

1. Press the Ctrl key of the keyboard and the centre key of the mouse (scroll wheel).
2. Keeping both pressed, move the cursor.

Drag the view

With the command:

1. Click on the button.
2. Press any mouse key, and keep it pressed to shift the display and drag the objectGeneral term used to define any drawing created in the drawing area (e.g. piece, texts, flat figures, etc.). in the required direction.

With the mouse:

1. Press the central mouse key (scroll wheel) to display the hand cursor.
2. Keep the central key (scroll wheel) pressed, then move the mouse to shift the display and drag the object in the required direction.

Enlarging and reducing the view

To maximise or minimise the view, follow the procedures described below.

Enlarge using the zoom commands

To enlarge or reduce the display, rotate the central mouse key (scroll wheel) forwards and backwards.

Alternatively, select the specific ZoomProcess of reducing or enlarging the graphic display. commands and click on the left mouse key.

Maximising a part of the drawing area

To perform this operation, click on the button, press the left mouse key, then position the cursor on the graphic and drag it to create a selection area.
