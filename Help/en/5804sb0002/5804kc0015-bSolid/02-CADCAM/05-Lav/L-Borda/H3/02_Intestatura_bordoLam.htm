Edgebanding strip trimming with blade

Description of the machining operation

Used to cut the edgebandingSet of operations for covering the edge of the piece (gluing, cleaning, rounding off, etc.). strip at the ends of the edgebanded side, using a blade.

Tool code

TNM

Predefined list of the blades in the databaseData bank. Stored data arranged in an organised manner so that they are readily available using a search and management program.. Choose the required one.

Reversal

RV

Enables (box tickedClick on a check box to tick it and thereby enable that function.) or disables a reversal of the machining direction.

To perform the machining operation, the aggregate(tool) Device fitted with tools. does not begin from the initial point of the profile, but from the end point. Only used if the profile has been generated in the anti-clockwise direction.

Linear offset 1

LOF

Movement A (expressed in millimetres) of the blade in relation to the cutting position on the first vertex (figure 2).

Linear offset 2

LO2

Movement D (expressed in millimetres) of the blade in relation to the cutting position on the second vertex (figure 2).

Angular offset 2

AO2

Tilt B of the blade in relation to the perpendicular in the cutting point on the second vertex (figure 3).

Vertex

CTT

Used to indicate which vertex (D and/or B, figures 2 and 3) the machining operation should be performed on, and therefore which data fieldsA defined area containing data or commands (data field or command field). should appear:

First

Used to fill in the "Linear offset 1" and "Angular offset 1" data fields in order to perform the operation on the first vertex.

Second

Used to fill in the "Linear offset 2" and "Angular offset 2" data fields in order to perform the operation on the second vertex.

Both

Used to fill in all four data fields indicated above, in order to perform the operation on both vertexes (B and D).

Corner

Allows to cut the edgebanded corner when applying very thin edges.

This type of machining operation is activated by clicking the iconGraphic image used to show a data item, button, etc. of the bulb (active) in column Corner of table "Machining settings".

Angular offset on corners

ANCRN

Allows to define the angle A of the tool starting from the perpendicular to the bisector B of the edge to be machined in order to slightly rotate the tool.

Basically, the tool works perpendicular to the bisector of the edge, which corresponds to the valuePredefined / pre-set. Standard information useful for the control, if the requested information is not entered. zero (rotation of 45 degrees).

Data check

EDC

Enables (box ticked) or disables the machine data check, to check the data tally with the programmed machining operation.

If the fieldA defined area containing data or commands (data field or command field). is disabled, the vigilance symbol is shown on the machining operation icon.

Chip cleaning blow

BFC

Enables (box ticked) or disables the activation of the blowers to remove woodchips from the edgebanding strips.

Reverse the cutting direction

ICR

Forces (box ticked) the blade to make the cut reversing its advance direction; the change of direction is made by rotating the blade 180 degrees (figure 6).

Whatever the machining direction programmed, the blade advance direction is reversed.

296. Representation of the data
Collision

CCC

Activates (box ticked) or deactivates the check on the moving parts and parts being machined, to avoid any possible interference or collision.

Working speed

WSP

Advance speed of the aggregate during the machining operation.

Cutting method

CTM

Reference point for the blade, for beginning the cut.

Central

Makes the cut using the central blade axis as its reference point; in this case, the blade makes the cut with its lower edge A (figure 7).

Peripheral

Makes the cut using the outer blade edge as its reference point; in this case, the blade makes the cut with its side edge B (figure 7).

297. Representation of the data
Blade radius correction

RTL

Blade radius correction when moving into and out of the edgebanding strip to be cut.

This value allows you to make a sham variation of the blade dimensions, to compensate any data inserted and to correct the position of the piece in relation to the position of the blade.

Descent start

VDE

Used to establish the initial point of the cut, where the blade increases its advance speed.

Descent end

QPV

Used to establish the final point of blade descent, at the end of the cut.
